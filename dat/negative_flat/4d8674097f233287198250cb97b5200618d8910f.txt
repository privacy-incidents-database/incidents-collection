Archives|Wielder of a Toy Gun Seized Near Ford Talk
October 31, 1975, Page 12  Archives
SAN FRANCISCO, Oct. 30 (AP)A man carrying a toy gun who said he was trying to protect President Ford was taken into custody today at a parking garage about a block from the downtown hotel where Mr. Ford spoke to a political luncheon.The man, who said his name was Albert W. Zero, was released to the San Francisco police after two hours. The Secret Service said there was no indication that he had intended to harm the President. The incident occurred about 10 minutes before Mr. Ford departed the St. Francis Hotel, Where five weeks earlier he had apparently been the target of an assassination attempt.Singing Glory, Glory Hallejulah and declaring an affection for whisky, the man was taken for questioning by Secret Service agents.The man said he pulled the gun after a garage employe had threatened Mr. Ford. The Secret Service said, however, there was no indication of such a threat.Threat to Ford ChargedSACRAMENTO, Calif., Oct. 30 (UPI) Thomas William Tillman, 32 years old, was held without bail today on a charge that he had threatened to kill President Ford with a gun in his possession, the Secret Service said.
