September 6, 1975, Page 26 The New York Times Archives
WASHINGTON, Sept. 5 (AP) of the four American Presidents slain in office, only John F. Kennedy was not physically close to his killer. The sniper's bullet that killed him was fired at his motorcade in Dallas, Tex., from a distance of 265 feet, according to the Warren Commission.On March 8, 1933, a sniper tried to kill Presidentelect Franklin D. Roosevelt at a political rally in Miami, but his longrange shot missed.In 1950, a group of Puerto Ricans tried to storm the Blair House in Washington, where president Truman was living. Mr. Truman was not hurt.The first attempt on an American President's life was in 1835, when a deranged painter named Richard Lawrence fired two pistols from about six feet away at President Andrew Jackson in the Capitol. Jackson was not injured.
Abraham Lincoln was killed in 1865 by a bullet fired from about a foot away while he was watching a play at Ford's Theater in Washington.President Garfield was mortally wounded by an officeseeker who fired at him from close range in a railroad station in Washington in 1881.President McKinley was fatally shot while shaking hands lin a reception line at an international exposition in Buffalo in 1901.President Theodore Roosevelt was shot in the chest as he left a Milwaukee hotel on Oct. 14, 1912. He survived.
