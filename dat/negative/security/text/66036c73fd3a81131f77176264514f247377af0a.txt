A broad communications network would reduce vulnerability to terrorism in the critical port region of New York and New Jersey, said a report issued on Wednesday by the Stevens Institute of Technology in Hoboken. The report, which was commissioned by the Smith Richardson Foundation of Westport, Conn., suggested that a single attack in the area could affect a broad swath of freight and transportation systems across the Northeast and threaten millions of residents. It proposed a network linking more than 30 agencies from all levels of government, along with private companies that help run the air and sea ports, to coordinate a faster and more effective response.
