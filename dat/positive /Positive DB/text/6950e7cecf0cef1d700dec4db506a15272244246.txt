Pacific Sunwear of California, which thrived in the 1990s and early 2000s as the purveyor of skate-and-surf cool, filed for bankruptcy protection on Thursday.Under a debt-for-equity restructuring agreement, the company plans to be taken private by Golden Gate Capital.Under the deal, Pacific Sunwear, also known as PacSun, will convert more than 65 percent of its debt into equity and receive at least $20 million in new capital from the private equity firm Golden Gate, the retailer said in a statement.PacSun, which has struggled especially with falling mall traffic, is expected to use its Chapter 11 bankruptcy to rein in its high store occupancy costs, either by shrinking its store footprint or renegotiating its rents. Its rapid growth in stores in the years leading up to the 2008 financial crisis has since weighed on PacSuns bottom line.PacSun, which once dressed teenagers in brands like Billabong and Quiksilver, has tried to refashion itself as edgier streetwear retailer.
Still, the retailer said it lost $10 million in the fourth quarter, compared with a net loss of $26 million over the same period the previous year. It said sales at stores open for at least a year edged up 0.2 percent in the fourth quarter. But for the full year, sales dipped 2.6 percent.For now, PacSun said its 601 stores would continue to operate without interruption to customers, employees or vendors.The retailer, which is based in Anaheim, Ca., filed for Chapter 11 in the federal bankruptcy court in Delaware early Thursday. The bankruptcy filing said that PacSuns assets ranged from $50 million to $100 million and that its liabilities were between $100 million and $500 million.Wells Fargo has agreed to provide a five-year, $100 million revolving credit facility once the retailer emerges from bankruptcy proceedings, PacSun said.PacSun joins a string of retailers that have filed for bankruptcy over the last year, including American Apparel and Quiksilver, as the industry struggles to keep up with changing consumer tastes and a shift to shopping online.An affiliate of Golden Gate Capital, whose portfolio also includes Eddie Bauer, Payless ShoeSource and California Pizza Kitchen, previously loaned PacSun $60 million.Wells Fargo has agreed to provide a five-year, $100 million revolving credit facility once the retailer emerges from bankruptcy proceedings, PacSun said.PacSuns chief executive, Gary H. Schoenfeld, said in a statement that the deal put the retailer in a very promising position as it seeks to overhaul both its brand and merchandising.The retailers shares had lost four-fifths of their value after Bloomberg reported on an impending bankruptcy earlier this week.
