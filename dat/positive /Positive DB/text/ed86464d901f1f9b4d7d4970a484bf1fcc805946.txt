IN the twenty-two papers which comprise this slim volume Sir William Beveridge identifies his celebrated social security plan as intended to be only one part of a comprehensive British program of social reform directed against, besides Want, the "four other giant evils of Disease, Ignorance, Squalor and Idleness."
